02 — Overview

Deal description

Spanish Villa (1940 Iglehart Avenue) and Autumn Manor (1940 Marshall Avenue) are two contiguous brick walkup apartment buildings in Saint Paul's Hamline-Midway corridor — 32 units each, 64 total. Built 1972 and 1967 respectively. Owned by the same family LLLP since acquisition, direct-from-seller transaction with no broker.

The two parcels share a property line, share utility infrastructure, share a tenant base, and have always been operated as one portfolio. We acquire them as one deal.

Hold philosophy
Our exit philosophy is to not exit. We build long-term portfolios that produce recurring monthly income into the future. Our preferred path is to refinance at Year 5 and Year 10, pull cash out tax-free where the rate environment permits, and keep operating the asset at a consistent cashflow. 10 — What could go wrong

Risks & constraints

A constraint to flag.

Year 1 DSCR comes in at 1.19, below the 1.25 standard agency threshold, and Year 1 cash-on-cash at 2.41% sits below our 3% floor. The deal becomes financeable and attractive by Year 3 mechanically, and the 4d enrollment lever moves it to comfortable by Year 2. We are proceeding on this constraint with the operator playbook explicitly committed.

If this constraint is one you would rather not own, opt out — there is no penalty for sitting this one out, and your capital comes back clean.

Year 1 financing tightness (override)

Year 1 DSCR comes in at 1.19 vs the 1.25 agency standard, and Year 1 cash-on-cash at 2.41% sits below our 3% floor. Both clear by Year 3 as rent growth outpaces expense growth, but the early window is structurally tight. We are proceeding on a documented exception, anchored on the 4d enrollment playbook in tile 2.

4d1 affordability enrollment is load-bearing

Underwritten annual property tax is $151,062. St. Paul's 4d program cuts the tax class rate from 1.25% to 0.25% on enrolled units (verified per MN Statute 273.13 subd. 25). Savings scale linearly with the % of units enrolled: 50% of units saves ~$58K/yr, 75% saves ~$87K/yr, 100% saves ~$115K/yr — beginning Year 2 of hold. At 100% the deal moves from 'below target' to 'comfortable.' All 64 units qualify under the 60% AMI ceiling and existing tenants grandfather from income verification at enrollment. Operator-execution dependent. Walk condition: 4d denial by HRA pre-LOI.

1972 / 1967 vintage capex pipeline

These are mid-century brick walkups. The seller's books show repairs at $1,542 per unit per year — about double the Class B norm. We carry a $200-400K reserve through Year 5. Walk condition: capex pipeline above $500K through Year 5 found during inspection.

Year 6 refinance rate risk

Financing is 5-year fixed term on a 30-year amortization, refinancing at Year 6. We underwrite the refi at the same 5.95% to stay conservative. If rates at Year 6 settle 50-100 bps higher, debt service rises and cash flow compresses just as the heaviest capex window opens. We negotiate for a 7-year fixed term during LOI to push refi past the capex pipeline.

Inner-ring St. Paul demand depth

Hamline-Midway and Frogtown trade wider on cap rate (6.25-7.50%) than core metro Class B because the buyer and renter pools are both narrower. We accept this in pricing and underwrite to a 1.5% market growth assumption rather than the metro 2.5%.

Long-tenured tenancy and mark-to-market friction

Rent roll shows $83K of embedded mark-to-market upside but several tenants have been in place since pre-2015. Long tenancy produces durable cash flow but in-place rents lag market and the gap closes slowly because tenants do not churn. We do not underwrite a mark-to-market lift in the base case.
